# Fonts

## Summary

Outfit + Switzer: A confident, modern pairing built for clarity. Outfit (headings) brings geometric warmth and approachability—ideal for establishing trust with business owners. Switzer (body) delivers neo-grotesk precision for effortless reading. Together, they communicate professional competence without corporate coldness. Use Outfit Bold for marketing impact, SemiBold for proposals and documents. Switzer Regular handles all body text; use SemiBold for inline emphasis. This pairing works across digital, print, and social.

## Do

HEADING HIERARCHY (Outfit)
• Title/Hero: Bold 700 at 72px+ for marketing, SemiBold 600 for proposals
• Subtitle: SemiBold 600 at 36-48px
• Heading: SemiBold 600 at 36px
• Subheading: Medium 500 at 28px
• Section header: Medium 500 at 20px
• Small heading: Regular 400 at 16px

BODY TEXT (Switzer)
• Lead paragraph: Regular 400 at 18px
• Body copy: Regular 400 at 16px
• Secondary text: Regular 400 at 14px
• Captions/labels: Medium 500 at 12px (extra weight aids small-size legibility)
• Quotes: Light Italic 300 at 18px

SPECIAL USES
• Social media hero text: Outfit ExtraBold 800 at 96px+
• Buttons/CTAs: Outfit SemiBold 600 at 14-16px
• Tags/badges: Switzer SemiBold 600 at 11-12px
• Inline bold: Switzer SemiBold 600 (never use Bold 700 in body)

GENERAL GUIDANCE
• Maintain generous line height: 1.4-1.6 for body, 1.1-1.2 for headings
• Keep heading line length under 20 words
• Body text line length: 50-75 characters for optimal readability
• Use Switzer Italic for emphasis within paragraphs, not bold
• Pair Outfit headings only with Switzer body—never mix heading fonts

## Don't

WEIGHT MISTAKES
• Don't use Outfit Thin/Light for headings—loses impact and authority
• Don't use Outfit Bold 700 for small text (under 20px)—gets heavy
• Don't use Switzer Bold 700 in body text—Medium 500 or SemiBold 600 max
• Don't mix Outfit weights randomly in the same heading hierarchy

PAIRING ERRORS
• Don't use Outfit for body text—it's wider and less optimized for reading
• Don't use other fonts alongside this pairing—breaks brand consistency
• Don't use Switzer for primary headlines—it lacks the geometric punch

FORMATTING ISSUES
• Don't set body text smaller than 14px
• Don't use ALL CAPS for body text (headings only, sparingly)
• Don't center-align long paragraphs
• Don't use Light Italic below 16px—loses legibility
• Don't skip heading levels (H1 → H3)—maintain hierarchy

STYLING PROBLEMS
• Don't underline text for emphasis—use Italic or SemiBold
• Don't use more than 3 heading sizes on a single page/design
• Don't stretch or compress the fonts—use only standard weights
• Don't apply drop shadows or outlines to body text

# Brand Voice

We're the smart engineer friend - calm, direct, confident, never pushy or arrogant. Outcomes over tools. Numbers over promises. Specific over vague: "saves 10 hours/week" beats "improves efficiency." No jargon or corporate buzzwords like "synergy" or "cutting-edge." Simple language anyone understands. We show, don't tell. Data-driven but human. We sound like we figured something out - not like a press release. We diagnose before prescribing, show proof and set clear boundaries.
